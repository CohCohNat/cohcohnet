Childhood Years

My name is Micoh G. Villar , and I was born at Fabella Hospital in Sta. Cruz Manila.1 Childhood for me was devoid of grandeur; it was a simple existence.2 Our household was a modest one, comprising my two grandparents, one of my aunties, my parents, and us, their four children.3 Among us siblings, we shared a unique bond: our names all began with the letter "M." This seemed to be our parents' way of forging a familial identity.4 Early memories take me back to Malabon City, where my grandparents and parents toiled, and where we called home.5 Despite their hard work, our lifestyle remained humble.6 Every penny was stretched to meet our basic needs and cover our bills.7 Yet, amidst financial constraints, education remained a priority.8 I commenced my schooling at Malabon Elementary School, encountering hardships from the outset.9 Bullied and unfairly reprimanded by teachers, I endured a traumatic phase, dreading the school's gates each morning.10 Eventually, the strain became too much, prompting my parents to halt my studies temporarily.11 Months later, tragedy struck as Typhoon Ondoy ravaged our home, forcing us to relocate to Cainta, Rizal.12

My parents enrolled me at Cainta Elementary School where I found the opportunity for a fresh start.13 Despite initial trepidation, I gradually found my footing, forming friendships that supported my academic journey.14 Soon, I emerged as one of the top performers in grades one and two.15 However, life's unpredictability soon intervened.16 Financial hardships compounded by my father's departure necessitated another move, this time to Pasig.17 Once again, I found myself in unfamiliar territory, adjusting to a new school and surroundings.18 Yet, armed with the resilience and determination cultivated during my early years, I navigated the challenges of elementary education with newfound confidence.19 With each hurdle overcome, I forged new friendships, absorbed fresh knowledge, and amassed experiences that would shape the trajectory of my future endeavors.20

Teenage Years

High school, for me, was an unforgettable chapter in my life, one filled with numerous experiences and achievements.1 I embarked on this educational journey at Manuel I. Santos Memorial National High School, now known as Manuel I. Santos Integrated School, situated in Taytay, Rizal.2 Securing a spot in this institution after successfully passing the entrance examination was a significant milestone.3 Moreover, I was placed in the first pilot section, a testament to my academic prowess.4 Transitioning into this new environment posed no challenge for me, given my prior experiences of switching schools.5 I adapted quickly, seamlessly integrating into the competitive atmosphere.6 Despite the intensity of academic competition, I remained committed to excelling and maintaining harmonious relationships with my peers.7

Throughout my high school tenure, I consistently demonstrated academic excellence, earning the distinction of being an honor student from start to finish.8 This achievement was a culmination of my dedication to my studies, camaraderie with classmates, and active participation in extracurricular activities.9 Among the cherished memories of my high school years is my involvement in the "Science Investigatory Project" team during grade 9.10 Leveraging my previous experiences from elementary school, I showcased my skills and competed at the Division Level, securing 2nd place in the Life Science individual category.11

My journey with the Science Investigatory Project continued into grade 10, presenting me with another opportunity to represent our school.12 Despite rigorous preparation and additional experiments based on judges' feedback, I fell short and attained 4thplace.13 Nonetheless, that experience served as a valuable learning opportunity and contributed to my personal growth.14 Furthermore, it provided me with the chance to publish my study in the Ascendans Asia Journal of Multidisciplinary Research Abstracts, a testament to the quality of my research.15

Simultaneously, I held the position of Vice President in our Supreme Student Government, collaborating with fellow leaders to organize various school activities and address student concerns.16 Additionally, I took on the role of Public Information Officer for the Taytay SSG Federation, extending my service to the larger Taytay community.17 These leadership roles allowed me to make a meaningful impact and hone my skills in governance and public relations.18

Reflecting on my accomplishments in high school fills me with a sense of fulfillment and anticipation for the future.19 As I prepare to embark on the next chapter of my journey, I carry with me the lessons learned and memories forged during these formative years.20

College Years

Amidst the finality of my high school years, the unexpected upheaval of the pandemic swept through, altering our educational landscape dramatically.1 Suddenly, everything transitioned to the digital realm, including our much-anticipated graduation ceremony.2 My well-laid plans were disrupted as I grappled with the decision of whether to pursue further studies or not, a choice made all the more challenging by the uncertainty of the times.3 Fortunately, Jose Rizal University offered late enrollment options, affording me the opportunity to continue my education.4 Senior high school unfolded predominantly in virtual classrooms, marking a period characterized by a blend of admiration and frustration towards online learning.5 Time seemed to slip away unnoticed, culminating in the unexpected arrival of graduation day, where farewells were bid to classmates without the customary shared experiences of traditional schooling.6

Despite the unconventional circumstances, I persevered, gaining invaluable insights into what awaited me in college.7 While senior high school lacked the traditional classroom dynamics, it served as a pivotal stepping stone toward my academic journey.8 Setting my sights on University of the East for college, I followed in the footsteps of my sisters and honored my father's collegiate alma mater, despite his unfinished tenure.9 The transition to UE proved to be a positive change, offering a more organized environment and streamlined processes.10

Embarking on my freshman year at UE filled me with excitement and anticipation.11 Delving into the realm of Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT), I found myself immersed in a world of new concepts and ideas.12 Forming connections with like-minded peers enriched my collegiate experience, fostering a sense of camaraderie and shared passion for learning.13 Each subject, whether minor or major, offered unique insights, shaped by the expertise and teaching styles of our professors.14

Throughout my freshman year, I delved into various programming languages, including Python, Java, C#, and R Languages, under the guidance of knowledgeable instructors.15 Their dedication to imparting knowledge fueled my curiosity and expanded my skill set exponentially.16 Additionally, I assumed the role of Executive Vice-President for E-sports within COMSSO, collaborating with a dynamic team of individuals and contributing to the vibrant campus community.17

Now embarking on my second semester of sophomore year, I recognize the boundless opportunities for growth and learning that lie ahead.18 As I strive to cultivate my skills and carve a path in the field of IT, I eagerly anticipate the challenges and discoveries awaiting me in the coming years.19 With a steadfast commitment to personal and academic development, I am poised to embrace the journey ahead with enthusiasm and determination.20
